---
title: Relationship Weights
---
It's interesting how we perceive each other in our heads. Say friendships and other relationships were represented as some sort of graph-like structure in our brains. Close family and friends would have relatively high weights in that graph, whereas acquaintances and people we recently met would have lower weights. Using this structure, I wonder what the people around me have as my weight relative to what I have for theirs. Do we value each other roughly the same? If not, who values the relationship more? Why would this be the case? How can we reach a better equilibrium to reflect both of our views on the relationship? This would be an interesting experiment to run, if it hasn't already been done.
