title: Adoption
---
Yesterday, there was a cyberattack on Canvas, which many colleges and universities use for courses to distribute information and grades. This caused the platoform to be unusable for several hours.

It is interesting how quickly people are to adopt technology that aims to improve some aspect of their lives (or not!). Canvas was launched in 2011 and 15 years later, is is a leading provider for learning management services. Obviously, there are much bigger pieces of technology (e.g. smartphones) that have adoption at wider scale, but nevertheless it fascinates me that there are a very small number of humans who know how these systems are built and can fix them in case of some major disruption (like yesterday). In a sense, a lof of technology is a black-box to most users and serves only the end-goal (which, of course is completely justified. It wouldn't make sense for evryone to be an expert on the iPhone's hardware or the nitty-gritty details behind the attention mechanism used in ChatGPT). 

Most of us use these systems without knowing how they work, which, for now, hasn't been a major problem (to my knowledge), but it might someday.
