---
title: Path Finding
---
You are likely at some stage in your life and have some goal of being at a different state in a few months/years. My mental model of how to achieve goals is to think of the knowledge you currently have as the tools you can use right now to get where you want. Along the way, you may also have to develop new tools and add these to your toolbox that work with your existing tools to reach your goal. This growth compounds, and over time you would reach your goal (or at least be reasonably close). 

For example, say you are a researcher want to publish paper(s) in top conferences. You likely have ideas on future papers you want to write, but require additional knowledge on how to implement these papers and execute on these ideas. You can simultaneously build this knowledge and work on your ideas to write these papers, which will hopefully be published and allow you to publish even more papers using your expanded knowledge base.
