---
title: Consistency
---
If you want to improve at something, consistency is vital. I strongly believe consistent effort over several days/weeks/months/years is better than short bursts of work. For example, in November, I started to read Andrew Ross Sorkin's *DealBook* newsletter, the Wall Street Journal's *Markets A.M.*, and Matt Levine's *Money Stuff* daily. I read the first two newsletters over breakfast and *Money Stuff* at night, usually over dinner or before sleeping. Since then, I have noticed my ability to read quickly, and recall information that I read (I actively try to practice this by trying to remember what was in a prior day or week's newsletter and checking how well I remembered this information. I typically do this when on a walk, on a bus or car ride, or right before sleeping). I have also noticed my understanding of financial markets, the global economy, deals, fiscal and monetary policy, and so many more topics improve significantly.

I want to implement this consistency and discipline in my life across several areas: fitness (specifically weightlifting and going to the gym) and reading technical textbooks and papers (this I already somewhat have, inertia from the academic year).

I have seen firsthand how consistency and discipline are the true symbols of freedom. I am the most focused and inspired when I am in a consistent routine.
