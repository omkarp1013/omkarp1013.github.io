---
title: The Deep Life
---
The title references Cal Newport's "Deep Life" movement, which somewhat relates to this post. Some of the most "successful" (in quotations because this is usually subjective. I am talking career-wise here.) people I know often think deeply about simple things. For example, I was at a restaurant with two friends a few weeks ago and we started to discuss context switching while in conversations, and how some people can flawlessly switch betweewn different topics. I had previously not thought this deeply about conversations unless they had some impact on another part of my life (such as an interview for a job or internship, for which I would prepare basic talking points for).

More generally, a good mental model I feel that people who think deeply question everything they read, listen to, or write, using their questions to break information down to first principles (atomic facts) and relating each fact to prior knowledge. I think this is a very useful skill to develop, as it has (so far) immensely improved my comprehension and ability to learn new topics. Mentally, I imagine somethng like a chain-of-thought reasoning trace (like in large language reasoning models) in my head, but this model might be visualized differently for everyone.
