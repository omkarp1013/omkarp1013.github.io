---
title: Pinnacles
---
It is a wonderful privilege to be born in a first-world country with a family that has sacrificed for your quality of life, education, and career/financial prosperity. I think about this very often, especially in the last year or so. Specifically, I've been thinking about how to repay my parents (and their parents) for these sacrifices (which I might talk about on another day). They have never asked for anything, nor do they expect anything. But it feels right to do so.

I strongly believe the ultimate repayment is in the form of being at the *pinnacle* of the most important things I invest my time into: my career (academic and professional), relationhships, and health. I want to make a name for myself in industry, I want to *thrive*. I want to have friends that I care deeply about[^1]. And finally, I want to be physically and mentally healthy.

These investments will have the most lucrative dividends, and are the core of what I consider essential to my overall well-being.

[^1]: And hopefully care deeply about me.
