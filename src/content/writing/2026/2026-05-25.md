---
title: Exigence
---
Starting now (technically, a few months ago), I want to make sure I have a strong reasons(s) and robust decision theory behind making a major decision[^1]. I think it is very important to understand why you are doing something and why you are *not* doing the other option(s) that you were presented with. Reasoning about this deeply and mapping out your options against your objective function(s) is a great thought exercise that may ultimately improve your decision-making abilities. Using this type of framework may also minimize the amount of regret that you would have when making a particular choice (the Regret Minimization Framework comes to mind here, and may be a part of this decision-making process). This process may sound simple, but I think it can be mentally taxing and really force one to think deeply about simple things (which is important!). In all, proving *exigence* for a particular choice when making a major decision is extremely important. 


[^1]: I'm not completely sure how a "major decision" may be defined, but usually it is something that would impact me for an extended period of time relative to other decisions I would make in the same timeframe. For example, choosing a college has significantly more implications than what I would eat for lunch on the same day.
