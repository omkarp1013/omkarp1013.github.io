---
title: Hours
---
Something I have learned by experience (although many have told me this before) is that the more *focused hours* you put into something, the better you will get at it (however "better" may be defined). Similarly, the more focused hours you put into something, the more edge you will have over others doing the same thing. I have noticed that the more focused hours I spend on something (e.g. reading research papers, reading about financial markets, etc.), the more brain space said topics occupy. Managing your brain space is extremely important, and is something I will probably write about in a future post, but I feel that you can (perhaps slowly) expand the amount of information your brain contains at any given time, and the more brain space something occupies and the more time you spend thinking about it, the better you will get at it.
