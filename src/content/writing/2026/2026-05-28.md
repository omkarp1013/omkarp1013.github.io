---
title: Triangles
---
I remember watching a TED talk in high school by a Harvard alumni and his experience as an undergraduate there. He described a triangle with the phrases "Social Life," "Grades," and "Sleep" at each vertex of the triangle, stating that at Harvard, it was only possible to achieve satisfaction in two of those areas. I realize now that this can be extended post-student life too, where you are working a job. Here, the vertices of the triangle contain "Health," "Money," and "Time." I feel that is is only possible to achieve satisfication in two of these three areas. Priotize your time and money, your health will likely take a hit. Prioritize your health and money, you will lose out on your free time (likely to preseve your health and make money). Finally, prioritize your time and health, you will likely spend your money doing so. I realize these are very strong generalizations, but basic observation tells me that this structure might loosely hold for a lot of people working in corporate America. I suppose balancing the three is also possible if you have enough money (you can't improve your health significantly after a certain point and nobody can get more time somehow), but you might have sacrificed a lot of time and possibly health to accumulate your wealth at an earlier stage in life.
