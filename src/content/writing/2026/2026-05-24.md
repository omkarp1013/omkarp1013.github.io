---
title: Intrinsic Motivation
---
The more I tend to think about something (or things) that I want to accomplish or work on, the more I tend to work on said things and actually accomplish what I wanted to. I think having an intrinsic desire/passion/willingness for something is an extremely strong and perhaps the best motivator. We often have extrinsic reasons to pursue things (money, prestige, power, perception are a few); while these reasons may be strong and worthwile, true intrinsic motivation is, I believe, more powerful than each of these because it draws from an innate desire for something. This desire may not even be completely interpretable, which I find fascinating.

For example, I love to read, specifically newsletters and books. I associate this passion with wanting to better understand the world that I live in. Newsletters and books are distillations of their authors' knowledge that they have accrued over a lifetime (or a sizeable part of their lifetime), so reading them can give one a tremendous amount of knowledge in a relatively small amount of time. But why should this be the case? Why should I want to understand the world? I could spend an infinite number of lifetimes reading and still not completely understand the world. So I do have an innate desire for reading, which I can't completely explain. But I still wake up every day most excited to read the newsletters I do, and the favorite parts of my day are often when I am reading before going to sleep.
