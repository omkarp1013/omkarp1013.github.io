---
title: Finality
---
There is something special about being near the end of something and *knowing it*. For example, it could be the end of high school or college, which is usually known well in advance. Or it could be the end of something that didn't have a known end date, like a relationship or job.

As I near the end of college, I realize that there is probably very little in the rest of my life that will be as well-defined as the schooling I have undergone in the last 16 years. When will my next relationship start and end? The next apartment lease? And so on and so forth. While writing, I'm reminded of my parents, who have experienced even more uncertainty regarding their career and education. 

I suppose, one goal from older generations to their children is to minimize this uncertainty. While managing uncertainty is a skill and should be developed, minimizing the amount of unnecessary uncertainty in one's life is certainly (no pun intended!) a worthwile pursuit. Finality is sometimes desired.
